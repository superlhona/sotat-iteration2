What is K-Means

The objective of K-means is simple: group similar data points together and discover underlying patterns. To achieve this objective, K-means looks for a fixed number (k) of clusters in a dataset. After identifying k number, K-means algorithm allocates every data point to the nearest cluster, while keeping the centroids as small as possible. A centroid is the imaginary or real location representing the center of the cluster.
